Signs You Have a Flea Problem

Flea infestations can escalate quickly, especially in warmer months. Here are the common signs to look for:

Itchy Bites on Ankles & Legs

Small, red, itchy bites typically clustered around the ankles, lower legs, and feet. Flea bites often appear in lines or groups of three.

Pets Scratching Excessively

Dogs and cats scratching, biting, or grooming themselves far more than usual. You may notice hair loss or red, irritated skin on your pet.

Flea Dirt in Bedding & Carpets

Tiny dark specks (flea droppings) in pet bedding, carpets, rugs, and soft furnishings. When placed on a damp white tissue, flea dirt leaves reddish-brown marks.

Seeing Fleas Jump

Spotting tiny dark brown insects (1–3mm) jumping on carpets, furniture, or your pet’s fur. Fleas can jump up to 150 times their own body length.

Why Flea Control Is Essential

Fleas are more than just a nuisance — they can affect the health of both you and your pets:

  • !Itchy, painful bites: Flea bites cause intense itching and discomfort. Some people experience significant swelling and irritation that lasts for days.
  • !Tapeworm transmission: Fleas can carry tapeworm larvae. If a pet swallows an infected flea during grooming, they can develop a tapeworm infection.
  • !Allergic reactions: Flea allergy dermatitis is one of the most common skin conditions in pets and can also affect humans who are sensitive to flea saliva.
  • !Secondary infections: Excessive scratching of flea bites can break the skin, leading to secondary bacterial infections that may require medical treatment.

Our Flea Treatment Process

1

Professional Spray Treatment

The Barron team applies a professional-grade insecticide treatment to all affected rooms, focusing on carpets, rugs, upholstered furniture, baseboards, and anywhere fleas and their eggs are likely to be hiding.

2

Pet Treatment Advice

We advise you to treat all pets in the household with a veterinary-approved flea treatment at the same time. Treating the property without treating pets will not resolve the problem.

3

Vacuuming Guidance

We provide detailed instructions on vacuuming routines to help activate flea pupae and bring them into contact with the treated surfaces. Regular vacuuming is essential for the treatment to work fully.

4

Follow-Up Visit

If activity persists beyond 2–3 weeks, we return for a follow-up treatment at no additional charge. Flea pupae can remain dormant for weeks, so patience and proper preparation are key.
