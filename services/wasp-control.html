Signs You Have a Wasp Problem

Wasp activity increases significantly during summer months. Here are the key signs to watch for:

Increased Wasp Activity

Large numbers of wasps, hornets, or yellowjackets flying around your yard or near your roofline, especially following a steady flight path to and from one location.

Visible Nest

A papery, gray-brown nest found in attics, under eaves, in sheds, garages, wall voids, or in the ground (yellowjackets love old rodent burrows). Mature nests can grow larger than a basketball.

Buzzing in Walls or Ceilings

A persistent buzzing or humming from inside walls, ceilings, or attics, indicating a hidden nest tucked into the structure of your building.

Wasps Entering Your Home

Wasps repeatedly finding their way indoors through small gaps, vents, or open windows, often a sign that a nest is close to or within the building.

Why Wasp Control Is Essential

Wasps become increasingly aggressive as summer progresses. Here’s why professional treatment is the safest option:

  • !Painful stings: Unlike bees, wasps can sting multiple times. Stings are painful and can cause significant swelling and discomfort lasting several days.
  • !Allergic reactions & anaphylaxis: For some people, wasp stings can trigger severe allergic reactions including anaphylactic shock, which can be life-threatening without immediate medical treatment.
  • !Aggressive when disturbed: Wasps release a pheromone when threatened that signals other wasps to attack. Disturbing a nest without professional equipment can result in mass stinging.
  • !Risk to children & pets: Children and pets are particularly vulnerable, as they may unknowingly disturb a ground-level nest or encounter wasps during outdoor play.

Our Wasp Treatment Process

1

Identify Nest Location

The Barron team locates the nest by observing flight paths and inspecting common nesting areas including attics, eaves, sheds, wall voids, and ground burrows.

2

Professional Insecticide Treatment

Using professional-grade insecticide powder or spray, we treat the nest directly. The product is carried back into the nest by returning wasps, ensuring complete colony elimination.

3

Nest Removal

Where accessible and appropriate, we remove the nest once activity has ceased. In some cases (such as inside wall voids), the nest is left to break down naturally after treatment.

4

Safety Zone & Advice

We advise you to keep away from the treated area for a few hours while the treatment takes full effect. We’ll provide guidance on preventing future nests on your property.
