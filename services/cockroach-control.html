Signs You Have a Cockroach Problem

Cockroaches are nocturnal and prefer to stay hidden. If you see one during the day, it often indicates a large infestation. Look for these signs:

Live Cockroaches

Spotting live cockroaches, especially at night in kitchens, bathrooms, and around heating systems. Seeing them during the day suggests a severe infestation.

Droppings

Small, dark cylindrical droppings resembling ground coffee or black pepper, found in drawers, behind appliances, along baseboards, and inside cabinets.

Egg Cases (Oothecae)

Brown, purse-shaped egg cases found in sheltered areas. Each case can contain up to 30–40 eggs, so even a few cases indicate a growing population.

Musty Smell

A distinctive, unpleasant musty or oily odor that becomes more noticeable as the infestation grows. This smell can taint food and surfaces.

Smear Marks

Dark, irregular smear marks on walls near floor level and along surfaces where cockroaches crawl, particularly in areas with water sources.

Why Cockroach Control Is Essential

Cockroaches are one of the most unhygienic pests you can encounter. Here’s why professional treatment is critical:

  • !Asthma & allergy triggers: Cockroach droppings, shed skins, and saliva contain allergens that can trigger asthma attacks and allergic reactions, particularly in children.
  • !Food contamination: Cockroaches crawl through drains, sewers, and waste before walking across food preparation surfaces, spreading bacteria and pathogens.
  • !Disease transmission: Cockroaches spread E. coli, salmonella, dysentery, and other serious bacteria. They contaminate everything they come into contact with.
  • !Reputational damage: For Long Island businesses — especially restaurants, delis, hotels, and food facilities — a cockroach infestation can trigger health department violations, closures, and lasting reputational damage.

Our Cockroach Treatment Process

1

Species Identification & Survey

The Barron team identifies the cockroach species (German, American, Oriental, or Brown-banded) to determine the most effective treatment approach. We also assess the extent of the infestation and harborage areas.

2

Gel Bait Treatment

Professional gel bait is applied to cracks, crevices, and harborage points. Cockroaches feed on the gel and carry it back to the nest, creating a cascade effect that eliminates the entire colony.

3

Residual Insecticide & Monitoring

We apply residual insecticide sprays to key areas and place sticky monitoring traps to track activity levels. This provides ongoing control and helps confirm when the infestation is cleared.

4

Follow-Up Visits

Cockroach infestations typically require multiple visits to ensure complete eradication, especially for German cockroaches. We return to check traps, reapply treatments, and confirm the problem is resolved.
