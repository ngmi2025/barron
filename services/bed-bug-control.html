Signs You Have a Bed Bug Problem

Bed bugs are expert hiders and feed mainly at night. Here are the telltale signs of an infestation:

Bites in Rows or Clusters

Itchy red bites that appear in lines or clusters on exposed skin, typically on arms, shoulders, neck, and face. Bites often appear overnight and may take days to show.

Blood Spots on Sheets

Small reddish-brown blood spots on your sheets, pillowcases, or pajamas caused by bed bugs being crushed after feeding, or from bites that bleed slightly.

Dark Spots on Mattress Seams

Small dark brown or black spots of bed bug excrement found along mattress seams, bed frames, headboards, and in the crevices of nearby furniture.

Sweet Musty Smell

A distinctive sweet, musty odor in the bedroom, often described as similar to almonds or cilantro. This scent becomes stronger as the infestation grows.

Shed Skins

Translucent, light brown shed skins (cast nymph skins) found near hiding spots. Bed bugs molt five times before reaching adulthood, leaving behind numerous shed skins.

Why Bed Bug Control Is Essential

Bed bugs can have a profound impact on your wellbeing and quality of life:

  • !Sleep disruption: Knowing bed bugs are present makes it extremely difficult to sleep. The anxiety and discomfort of being bitten during the night leads to chronic sleep deprivation.
  • !Psychological distress: Bed bug infestations cause significant stress, anxiety, and embarrassment. Many people experience ongoing distress even after the infestation has been treated.
  • !Skin infections: Excessive scratching of bed bug bites can break the skin, leading to secondary bacterial infections that may require antibiotics.
  • !Rapid spread: Bed bugs can spread quickly to other rooms and neighboring apartments through luggage, clothing, and furniture. Early treatment prevents a small problem from becoming a major infestation.

Our Bed Bug Treatment Process

1

Thorough Inspection

The Barron team performs a detailed inspection of all bedrooms and surrounding areas to confirm bed bug presence, identify the extent of the infestation, and locate every harborage point.

2

Heat or Chemical Treatment

Depending on the situation, we use professional insecticide sprays applied to mattresses, bed frames, furniture, and all crevices, or heat treatment to raise room temperatures to lethal levels for bed bugs.

3

Preparation Guidance

We provide detailed instructions on washing bedding at high temperatures, decluttering, and other steps to maximize treatment effectiveness and prevent re-infestation.

4

Multiple Follow-Up Visits

Bed bug treatments typically require 2–3 visits spaced 7–14 days apart to catch newly hatched nymphs. We continue until we are confident the infestation is completely eliminated.
