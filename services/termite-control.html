Signs You Have a Termite Problem

Termites work quietly behind walls and below floors, often for years before homeowners notice. Here are the warning signs Long Island homeowners should watch for:

Mud Tubes

Pencil-thin tubes of dirt running up foundation walls, crawl space piers, or basement walls. Subterranean termites build these tunnels to travel safely between soil and wood.

Spring Swarmers

Winged reproductive termites (swarmers) appearing inside your home, typically in spring after a warm rain. Discarded wings on windowsills or near light fixtures are a classic red flag.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Wall studs, baseboards, door frames, or floor joists that sound hollow when tapped. Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving only a thin outer shell.

Sagging or Damaged Wood

Floors that feel spongy, sagging ceilings, buckling baseboards, or blistering paint that looks like water damage. These are signs of advanced termite activity inside structural wood.

Frass & Debris

Small piles of what looks like sawdust or coffee grounds near baseboards, window frames, or attic wood. Tight-fitting doors and windows that suddenly jam can also signal termite activity.

Why Termite Control Matters

Termites are the single most destructive wood-destroying insect in North America. Here’s why early, professional treatment is critical for Long Island homes:

  • !Structural damage: Eastern subterranean termites eat 24/7 and can silently compromise floor joists, sill plates, and load-bearing studs. Repair bills can easily run into tens of thousands of dollars.
  • !Not covered by insurance: Most homeowners policies exclude termite damage because it’s considered preventable. That makes proactive inspections and protection plans essential.
  • !Property value: Active termite infestations can derail home sales and appraisals. A documented Barron protection plan gives buyers and lenders confidence.
  • !They don’t stop: A single mature colony can contain hundreds of thousands of workers. Without treatment, the damage compounds season after season.

Our Termite Control Process

1

Thorough Inspection

Our technicians perform a detailed inspection of your foundation, crawl space, basement, attic, and exterior, looking for mud tubes, active galleries, moisture issues, and conducive conditions around the structure.

2

Liquid Soil Treatment

We apply non-repellent liquid termiticide (such as Termidor) around the perimeter of your home, creating a continuous treated zone that eliminates foraging termites and protects against new colonies.

3

Baiting Systems

For properties where liquid treatment isn’t practical, we install in-ground baiting systems (such as Sentricon) that eliminate entire colonies and provide year-round monitoring around the structure.

4

Annual Protection Plan

We back our treatments with an annual protection plan that includes yearly inspections, monitoring, and a service warranty, so your home stays protected long after the initial treatment.
