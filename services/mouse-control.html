Signs You Have a Mouse Problem

Mice are small and elusive, but they leave plenty of evidence behind. Look out for these common signs:

Small Droppings

Tiny dark droppings, shaped like grains of rice (about 1/4 inch long), found in cabinets, pantries, along walls, and near food sources.

Gnaw Marks

Small, neat gnaw marks on food packaging, furniture, baseboards, and electrical wiring. Mice gnaw constantly to keep their teeth short.

Nesting Materials

Shredded paper, fabric, attic insulation, and other soft materials gathered into nests, often found in hidden, warm areas.

Urine Pillars & Smell

In heavy infestations, mouse urine can build up into small mound-shaped “urine pillars” made of grease, dirt, and urine. A strong ammonia smell is also common.

Scratching in Walls

Scratching, scurrying, or rustling sounds in walls, ceilings, and crawl spaces, particularly at night when mice are most active.

Why Mouse Control Is Essential

Mice may be small, but they cause big problems. Here’s why professional treatment is important:

  • !Disease transmission: Mice carry hantavirus, salmonella, and other dangerous pathogens. Their urine and droppings contaminate surfaces and food stores.
  • !Food contamination: Mice urinate constantly as they move, contaminating food preparation areas and stored goods, creating serious hygiene risks.
  • !Property damage: Mice gnaw on electrical wiring, plumbing, insulation, and framing lumber. Damage to wiring is a significant fire risk.
  • !Rapid breeding: A single female mouse can produce up to 10 litters per year, with 5–6 young per litter. Populations can explode in weeks if left untreated.
