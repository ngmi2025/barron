Pest Library

Not sure what pest you’re dealing with? Browse our pest library to identify common UK pests, learn about the signs of infestation, and find out what professional treatment involves.

🐀

Rats

Brown rats (Norway rats) are the most common rat species in the UK. They carry diseases including leptospirosis and salmonella, gnaw through cables and pipes, and contaminate food stores.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Dark, pellet-shaped droppings (10–20mm)
  • • Gnaw marks on wood, plastic or cables
  • • Scratching noises at night
  • • Greasy rub marks along walls
Treatment Options
🐁

Mice

House mice can squeeze through gaps as small as 6mm and breed rapidly — a single pair can produce up to 2,000 offspring per year. They contaminate food and cause damage by gnawing.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Small droppings (3–8mm), rice-shaped
  • • Ammonia-like smell in enclosed areas
  • • Shredded paper or fabric (nesting)
  • • Small gnaw marks on food packaging
Treatment Options
🐝

Wasps

Wasps build nests in lofts, wall cavities, sheds and trees. A mature nest can contain over 10,000 wasps. They become aggressive when disturbed and their stings can cause serious allergic reactions.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Increased wasp activity around your property
  • • Visible nest (papery, grey/brown)
  • • Wasps entering/exiting a hole in wall or roof
  • • Buzzing sound from loft or cavity
Treatment Options
🪳

Cockroaches

German and Oriental cockroaches are the most common species in the UK. They spread bacteria including E. coli and salmonella, trigger asthma and allergies, and are a serious hygiene concern in commercial kitchens.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Musty, unpleasant odour
  • • Droppings resembling ground coffee
  • • Egg cases (oothecae) in dark crevices
  • • Live insects seen at night
Treatment Options
🦟

Fleas

Cat and dog fleas are the most common in UK homes. They bite humans and pets, causing itchy welts. Flea pupae can lie dormant in carpets for months, hatching when they detect vibration and warmth.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Itchy bites, often around ankles/legs
  • • Pets scratching excessively
  • • Tiny dark specks in pet bedding (flea dirt)
  • • Small jumping insects on soft furnishings
Treatment Options
🛏️

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs are small, flat, reddish-brown insects that feed on human blood at night. They hide in mattress seams, bed frames and furniture cracks. Infestations are not related to cleanliness and can affect anyone.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Itchy bites in lines or clusters
  • • Small blood spots on bedding
  • • Dark faecal spots on mattress seams
  • • Sweet, musty odour in severe cases
Treatment Options
🐦

Birds

Feral pigeons and gulls cause significant property damage through fouling and nesting. Bird droppings are acidic and can degrade stonework, block gutters, and pose a health risk through diseases such as psittacosis.

Signs of a Problem

  • • Large amounts of bird droppings
  • • Nesting materials in gutters or on roof
  • • Blocked drains or guttering
  • • Noise from roosting birds
Treatment Options
🪵

Woodworm

Woodworm is the larval stage of wood-boring beetles. The larvae tunnel through timber for several years, weakening structural beams, floorboards and furniture. Common in older Long Island properties.

Signs of Infestation

  • • Small round exit holes (1–2mm)
  • • Fine powdery dust (frass) below holes
  • • Crumbling or weakened timber
  • • Small beetles near windows in spring
